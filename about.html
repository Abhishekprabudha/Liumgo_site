About Lium Go

Lium Go exists to make electric logistics operationally viable for enterprises, not just a pilot project. We combine network design, operations discipline and EV expertise into one consistent delivery fabric.

From EV pilots to a production-grade network

Many enterprises start EV adoption through small city pilots. The challenge is scaling beyond a few stores or lanes while keeping SLAs and cost structures intact.

Lium Go was formed to close this gap. The team brings experience across logistics, fleet operations, technology and city-scale network planning. Every new site and city is onboarded using repeatable playbooks.

  • Standardised launch checklist for new cities and hubs
  • Benchmark SLAs and cost envelopes by sector
  • Structured governance between client, Lium Go and fleet partners

Lium Go at a glance

  • Multi-modal EV fleet: 2W, 3W and 4W
  • Dedicated playbooks for retail, grocery, Q-commerce and pharma
  • Control tower framework for real-time visibility and escalation
  • Data capture to support sustainability reporting and audits

Operating model

Lium Go works as a network orchestrator. Instead of owning every asset, we align city-level partners, riders and infrastructure under one governance and technology layer.

  • Fleet partners are onboarded against common SOPs and scorecards
  • Rider safety, productivity and training are centrally defined
  • Performance is reviewed in structured weekly and monthly forums

What we optimise

The network is optimised not just for cost per delivery, but across a wider set of metrics:

  • On-time performance, first-attempt success and rider productivity
  • Battery utilisation, charging downtime and vehicle health
  • Customer experience, NPS and store-level feedback

Culture & governance

Logistics is a reliability business. Lium Go emphasises transparent reporting, predictable governance and actionable operations reviews instead of vanity metrics.

  • Daily operations dashboards shared with client teams
  • Clear accountability for issue resolution and root-cause analysis
  • Structured roll-out of new features, lanes and geographies

Partnership philosophy

We prefer multi-year partnerships over short-term spot operations. This allows co-investment into hubs, technology integrations and fleet planning that reduces cost and risk over time.

If you’re evaluating EV logistics over the next 12–36 months, Lium Go can help build a roadmap from pilot to scaled operations.
