Services & operating models

Lium Go offers modular EV logistics services that can be combined into a tailored operating model for your network, volumes and SLAs.

Core delivery services

Same-day & next-day last mile

EV fleets executing same-day and next-day deliveries within defined service areas, tightly integrated to your order management and WMS systems.

  • Configurable cut-offs, slot windows and dispatch cadence
  • Wave planning aligned to warehouse throughput
  • Address and PIN code level eligibility mapping

Dedicated brand fleets

White-labelled delivery fleets and riders mapped exclusively to your brand or region, with custom uniforms, training and process nuances.

  • Brand-specific SOPs embedded into training
  • Dedicated rider cohorts for high-value customers
  • Visibility into roster and allocation at hub level

Cold chain & sensitive cargo

Temperature-controlled and sensitive cargo flows operated on EVs with monitoring, exception handling and compliance processes.

  • Device integrations for temperature and location
  • Cold box handling SOPs and route planning
  • Sealed chain-of-custody for audits

Reverse logistics & returns

Pick-up and consolidation flows for returns, buy-backs and recycling using the same EV network to minimise cost and emissions.

  • Pick-up slot scheduling for B2C returns
  • Route design for B2B collection from stores
  • Segregation and routing to central hubs

Network & design services

Hub & service area design

Jointly designing hubs, micro hubs and service areas so EV fleets can deliver within time windows while maintaining utilisation and battery health.

  • Analysis of current lanes, volumes and order density
  • Hub catchment modelling and fleet sizing
  • Playbooks for migration from ICE to EVs

Charging & energy strategy

Planning charging and swapping strategies that minimise downtime while supporting multi-shift operations, including partner selection and SLAs.

  • On-site vs. near-site vs. public charging mix
  • Turnaround time and queuing benchmarks
  • Charging SLAs integrated into dispatch decisions

Engagement models

Pilot

1–2 cities, limited store and hub footprint. Used to validate service quality, economics and integration approach.

  • Clearly defined success metrics
  • Short feedback cycles and design iterations
  • Exit and expansion options documented upfront

Scale-up

Multi-city roll-out with standardised processes, performance governance and co-branded EV fleets where needed.

  • Phased city and hub onboarding plan
  • Central programme office support
  • Quarterly joint optimisation reviews

Strategic partnership

Long-term network and fleet co-investment with volume commitments and joint planning for 3–5 years.

  • Shared roadmap for new services and lanes
  • Volume-linked commercials and incentives
  • Shared sustainability and reporting objectives
